The Myth of Good Web Design

How perfectly great designs go bad

Why did that amazing Dribbble design fall flat when it made it to production? How did all that UX research, prototyping, and testing produce... this? It's the user's fault right? They're always messing up my beautiful designs.

Or maybe it's you, the designer, that had access to all these resources and completely misjudged your customers. You have access to copywriters, a subscription to Creative Cloud, and 1000 designer friends following you on social media. Your customers have an aging smartphone, two screaming toddlers, and not enough time to put up with your required fields nonsense. You designed a service for yourself.
